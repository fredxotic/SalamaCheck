Safety Resources & Action Guide

If SalamaCheck detected a high-risk threat, please prioritize your safety. This guide provides immediate steps and international resources.

Immediate Action Steps

If you feel in danger, call emergency services immediately.

This tool is not a substitute for law enforcement. If a threat is physical or immediate, contact your local emergency number.

Document Everything Take screenshots and save the entire message content. Do not delete or edit evidence.
Do Not Engage Block the sender and do not respond to messages or click suspicious links.
Report Immediately Use the resources below to report threats to relevant platforms or authorities.

Digital Safety Helplines

Cyber Safety & Harassment

Resources dedicated to combating cybercrime, stalking, and online abuse.

Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3)
Mental Health & Crisis

If the threat involves self-harm or severe distress.

IASP Global Crisis Centres Directory
Online Child Safety

If the threat involves a minor or suspected grooming/exploitation.

National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC)
Phishing & Fraud Reporting

For reporting malicious links, phishing attempts, or digital scams.

Anti-Phishing Working Group (APWG)

Report to Online Platforms

Most platforms have dedicated teams for reviewing harmful content. Use their built-in reporting features:

Facebook/Instagram

Use the "Report Post" or "Report Profile" feature directly within the app.

Twitter/X

Use the "Report Tweet" or "Report Profile" options in the platform.

Discord/Gaming

Report using the platform's Trust & Safety form, providing User IDs and message links.

WhatsApp/Messaging

Use the built-in reporting feature and block the contact immediately.

Remember Your Safety Comes First

Trust your instincts. If a situation feels unsafe, remove yourself from it and seek help immediately. Digital threats can have real-world consequences - take them seriously.
